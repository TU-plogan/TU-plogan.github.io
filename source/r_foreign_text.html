Foreign Text

Tagging words or phrases in a different language.

Words or phrases which are not in the main language of the text should be tagged as such, at least where the fact is indicated in the text. Such tagging helps to differente among the different functions for the use of italics, for example, by identifying when italics is used simply to indicate a foreign word.

Where the word or phrase concerned is already distinguished from the rest of the text by virtue of its function (for example, because it is a title, a technical term, a quotation, a mentioned word, etc.) then the global @xml:lang attribute should be used to specify additionally that its language distinguishes it from the surrounding text. Any element in the TEI scheme may take an @xml:lang attribute, which specifies both the writing system and the language used by its content. Where there is no other applicable element, the element <foreign> may be used to provide a peg onto which the @xml:lang may be attached.

Source: https://www.tei-c.org/release/doc/tei-p5-doc/en/html/CO.html#COHQHF.

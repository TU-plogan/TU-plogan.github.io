Order Footnote Areas

Footnotes are always the last areas on the page.

Regardless of whether the occur at the end of the first or the second column, footnote areas always output after all of the text on a page. Think of the two text columns as the main text in one continuous flow that should not be interrupted by notes. And consider the footnotes areas as a second continuous flow of text, following the main text.

On a page with notes at the bottom of two continuous columns, the main text will be areas 1-2, and the notes will be areas 3-4.
A page with notes only at the bottom of the first column, the main text will be areas 1-2, and the notes will be area 3, as in the example below.

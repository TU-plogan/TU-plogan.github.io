Editorial standards

The following editorial principles are employed in creating this digital edition.

The sources texts we edit contain many different kinds of material, including multiple languages, scientific formulas, illustrations, tables, footnotes, marginal subheadings, and so forth. These standards evolved with the goal in mind of creating as accurate a reproduction of the encyclopedia text as possible and allowing for the incorporation of further details at a later date, if desired. This list describes the essential principles adhered to in creating this edition.

  1. Diacritics
    • All original language characters with their diacritical markings in the source text are reproduced. Some languages, such as Hebrew and Sanskrit, require special treatment and are noted for further review during the OCR stage, instead of corrected initially.
  2. Footnotes
    • All footnote text is preserved and moved inline with the body text, at the site of the original footnote anchor.
    • Source note numbers are replaced with a continuous sequence of note numbers restarting with one for each entry.
  3. Formulas
    • Simple one-line math and chemical formulas are reproduced. Complex multiline formulas cannot be reproduced by OCR and are outside the scope of the project.
  4. Illustrations
    • Images are outside the scope of the edition.
  5. Headings
    • Subheadings in the margins or in cut-outs of the text area are not included.
    • Headings and subheadings within the normal flow of the text are included.
  6. Page Layout
    • The two-column format of individual pages is not preserved. All type is presented as a single column.
    • Page breaks are indicated in place in the metadata.
    • Column breaks are not indicated.
    • Table data is preserved in tabular format, but no attempt is made to reproduce the visual attributes of the source table.
  7. Source errors
    • Obvious misspellings in the source text are not corrected.
    • Illegible text is rare. When it occurs, it is indicated as follows: [...].
  8. Spelling
    • Original spelling is prserved throughout.
  9. Type style
    • The following font formatting is preserved:
      • Italics
      • Small Caps
      • Bold
      • Underscore
    • The following formatting is ignored:
      • Typeface
      • Font size
